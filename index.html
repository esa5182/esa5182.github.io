About Me

Hi, my name is Eisa Aldhaheri, and I am a student at Penn State planning to major in Data Science. I am passionate about leveraging technology and data to solve real-world problems and improve the way individuals and organizations operate. Throughout this course, I developed a strong interest in Information Sciences and Technology (IST) because it bridges the gap between technical systems and human interaction. What stands out to me about IST is its focus on applying technology in meaningful, practical ways while also emphasizing communication, collaboration, and problem-solving skills. While IST may not dive as deeply into programming theory as Computer Science, it offers a broader and more holistic perspective on how technology integrates into everyday life and business environments. This well-rounded approach has strengthened my understanding of how to use technology effectively, not just build it, and has further motivated me to pursue a career in data-driven decision-making.

Course Overview

IST 110: Information, People, and Technology explores how information systems and technologies are used to organize, support, and improve human activities and organizations. The course focuses on three main perspectives: information (how data is stored and processed), people (how users interact with technology), and technology (how systems are designed and function).

Throughout the semester, I engaged in a wide range of activities including discussions, quizzes, individual assignments, group work, and a final portfolio project. Topics covered included binary systems, data analytics, hardware, operating systems, programming languages, data organization, the internet, cybersecurity, privacy, and business applications of technology.

This course emphasized a hands-on, problem-solving approach where I learned by actively working on real-world scenarios, collaborating with peers, and applying concepts to practical tasks. It helped me develop critical thinking, research skills, and the ability to evaluate how technology impacts individuals and organizations.

By the end of the course, I gained a stronger understanding of how technology integrates into everyday life, how to analyze and use digital systems effectively, and how to anticipate the impact of emerging technologies on society and business environments.

Discussion Posts
